Pain

As humans become less active and adopt more sedentary lifestyles revolving around screens, the threat of muscoskeletal pain becomes more and more urgent. If left unchecked, these can often develop into chronic pain, which is long standing pain that persists beyond the usual recovery period. Pain can be extremely taxing, to the point that affected individuals can't work, eat properly, take part in physical activity, or enjoy life.

Some of the most common types of pain that can result from technology use are:

  • Hand pain (osteoarthritis, De Quervain's tenosynovitis, trigger finger)
  • Hand numbness and tingling (carpal tunnel syndrome, cubital tunnel)
  • "Tech neck" (neck and shoulder pain/stiffness)
  • Lower back and hip pain
  • Eye strain

Pain recovery can be a long and painful process, especially if done incorrectly. Visit the subpages in this section to learn about proper rehabilitation techniques.
