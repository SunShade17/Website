Posture

The most important step to avoiding/minimizing pain from technology is to have good posture

Computer Workstation Ergonomics

It is worth it to invest in a proper workstation that minimizes stressors on your body. This means that your neck isn't contorted/bent back or down, your arms aren't lifted or extended out to the sides, your wrists and hands are bent upwards or sideways, and your spine isn't twisted. Here is an expert-approved checklist for an ergonomic workspace:

  • Eye level at the top of the screen or 1-2 inches below
  • A display monitor roughly an arm's length away
  • An ergonomic chair with lumbar support, reclined at ~100 degrees
  • Elbows bent at 90 degrees
  • A desk set at the proper height (wrists flat on keyboard), or a standing desk
  • A comfortable external keyboard
  • An ergonomic mouse that fits the hand
  • Knees level with hips or slightly below
  • Feet flat on the ground

Although it may be troublesome to follow this rigorously, it is worth the effort. At the least, follow as many steps as possible, and try to keep sitting posture in mind.

In addition, remember to not sit for prolonged periods, and try to get up and walk around at least once per hour. Following these habits will ensure that your body doesn't get stiff and remains healthy.

Phone Posture

Numerous studies have linked phone use to pain and dysfunction. Prolonged device usage can cause forward neck posture, slouched posture, or rounded shoulders, which can result in injury to the structures of the neck and spine. Here are some healthy habits to build while using mobile phones:

  • Stand or sit upright, don't slouch or slump
  • Keep phone at eye level
  • Minimize the amount that the arms are floating in front of body
  • Find a place to rest elbows (opposite hand, ribs, a desk, knees, etc.)
  • Try using voice dictation instead of texting

Eye Protection

One of the most common adverse physical health impacts from digital devices is eye strain, resulting in discomfort, fatigue, dry eye, blurry vision, and headaches

Luckily, there are many ways to prevent/treat eye strain, such as:

  • Follow the 20/20/20 Rule: Every 20 minutes, look at something at least 20ft away, for at least 20 seconds
  • Enlarge text on screens
  • Have proper lighting and avoid glare
  • Turn on the blue light filter / use glasses that offer blue light protection
  • Keep the screen about an arm's length away
  • Blink often
  • Use lubricating drops or prescription medicine for severe cases
