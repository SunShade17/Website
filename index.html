Technology harms your body

And far more than you think

In a world with tens of billions of internet connected devices, avoiding technology in daily life is impossible. While life is easier and more convenient than ever due to technology, this has resulted in the excessive use of screens in daily life and the neglect of physical health. While technology has also been linked with many adverse psychological effects like anxiety and depression, this site will focus on the physical impacts, which often go unrecognized, despite being wide-ranging and ubiquitous.

For instance, over 500 million people globally suffer from low back pain, 60% of Americans are affected by digital eye strain, and 1.1 billion young people worldwide are at risk of hearing loss from unsafe listening habits. Furthermore, research has shown technology to cause an array of other problems, including musculoskeletal issues, disrupted sleep, delayed digestion, stress, obesity, neurological impairments, developmental defects in children, cardiovascular disorders including cancer and heart disease, and more.

The problem is growing

Technology will only become more and more engrained in our lives

The global average screentime is 6 hours 58 minutes per day, an increase of 50 minutes per day since 2013. Extrapolated across all global internet users, that's over 180 million additional days. This is even worse among younger people, who average 9 hours of screentime per day. With this, the negative impacts of technology will be exacerbated. For instance, while 500 million people suffer from low back pain currently, the number of cases will increase to 850 million by 2050, with a similar trend for other risks.

Why care?

Chances are, you are already affected, or will be soon.

Pain hurts

If left unmanaged, chronic pain can quickly escalate and consume your life

Costly obstacle

Chronic pain lowers productivity and increases healthcare costs. For example, back pain alone costs the US economy $635B annually!

Recovery is hard

It's often very hard to fully recover from chronic pain - don't take your health for granted

Serious conditions

Poor physical health increases the risk of severe conditions like diabetes, heart disease, and cancer

Developmental issues

Unhealthy technology usage habits have been shown to impair children and adolescent development

Personal growth

On the other hand, taking care of your physical health leads to wellbeing, self-confidence, and a vibrant lifestyle
